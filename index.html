02 — Featured project

Telecom Customer Churn Prediction

Supervised ML · imbalanced classification · tabular

Predict which telecom customers are about to leave — early enough to act — while keeping the model interpretable enough to name the churn drivers. Retaining a customer costs 5–10× less than acquiring one, so a reliable risk flag is a direct lever on profit.

Approach

  • Cleaned and encoded the IBM Telco dataset (~7k customers, 21 features); MinMax-scaled, with SMOTE to fix the class imbalance.
  • Feature selection via chi-squared (categorical) and ANOVA F-test (numerical); PCA tried and rejected — it hurt ROC-AUC and broke interpretability.
  • Benchmarked eight classifiers (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, Random Forest, and more) plus a stacking ensemble, scored with repeated stratified CV.
  • Built a Keras ANN (19→8→4→1) and an AutoKeras search; compared four optimizers across up- and down-sampling, then tuned the best models with GridSearch.

Results reported in the Colab notebook

CatBoost (tuned) 86%
Keras ANN (up-sampled) 86%
XGBoost 84.5%

Once the imbalance was handled and the boosted model tuned, a compact 3-layer network matched the best tree ensemble rather than beating it — a useful finding for a tabular problem this size.
